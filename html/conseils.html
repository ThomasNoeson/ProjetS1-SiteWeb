CV writing tips

The aim of a CV is to get you a job interview and a job so this is an indispensable and necessary document. Here are a few advice to write your CV.

MANDATORY SECTION

  • Education

A formation inform the recruiter about your competencies development environment. His main objective is to find candidat's competencies. The writing of this section is chronological. The last studies must be the first to appear so the recruiter sees all your diplome, and the most important in first.

  • Skills

Put in this section all the competencies that can you sell you to a recruiter. Indicate especially the skills that are useful for the job that you want.

  • Languages

English, Spanish, Italian, German ? Indicate all foreign language that you can. Indicate also your stay abroad or year abroad but not your Holidays. After judge your level between bilingual and notion. Attention, some recruiter can impose a part of the interview job in an other language.

  • Hobbies

In this section, you include all that will allow to the recruiter to know you better. Write this part in focusing your content to necessary competecie in a company.

LAYOUT

Your name, contact details, must be at the top of your CV, with a picture but not mandatory. It's better neither photo that an holidays cut photo. The design is as you want but the CV must give a first impression, so make a CV as you are.
